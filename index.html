Dear Accenture Recruiting Team,

I am writing to express my keen interest in the Strategy & Consulting Internship within the Resources Industry practice, preferably in Amsterdam.

I am currently in the transition phase between my Bachelor of Science in Business Psychology and beginning a Master's degree in Work and Organizational Psychology. During my studies, my focus has strongly been on change management and the underlying reasons why large-scale transformations often fail. I graduated with top marks for my bachelor's thesis on “Communicative Methods and Strategies in Transformation Processes.” Through my research, I concluded that structural changes, such as the massive sustainability and digital shifts currently reshaping the energy and industrial sectors, can only succeed if the human element is methodically integrated. I am now eager to broaden my horizon, step beyond pure organizational psychology, and dive deeply into the broader strategic and operational challenges your clients face in the Resources Industry.

Accenture’s philosophy of putting humans at the center of digital and sustainable transformations perfectly aligns with my belief that strong strategy and psychology must go hand in hand. I am particularly drawn to the Amsterdam office as an innovation hub for green transformation. I am highly motivated to learn how these complex strategies are developed, how market analyses are conducted, and how they are ultimately executed on a large scale.

Over the past three years as a working student at cbs consultancy, I have honed my ability to manage complex projects and stakeholder interactions. Organizing formats ranging from small meetings to large-scale events with nearly 1,000 participants has equipped me with the organizational and communicative skills essential for assisting with client workshops and interviews in a fast-paced environment.

Furthermore, during an internship at Peng Peng! Consultancy in the summer of 2025, I actively supported change projects by performing research, designing workshops, and translating complex insights into clear, actionable storylines for clients. These experiences have sharpened my analytical and problem-solving abilities, which I am eager to apply and further develop within your Strategy team. I would be delighted to bring my unique perspective, strong drive to learn, and hands-on mentality to your projects during a three-month internship starting on June 15. Since my application relies on my CV and letters of recommendation rather than a transcript of records, I look forward to the opportunity to personally convince you of my academic and practical capabilities in an interview.

I look forward to hearing from you.
